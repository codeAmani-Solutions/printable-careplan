Kay's Care Plan

A resource for consistent, person-centered support.

About Kay

Kay is a 74 year old female, who lives in Doctor Philips with her supportive husband Bob and their fur baby Missy.

She is vibrant, expressive, and able to make her needs known.

Kay wears her eyeglasses at all times. As Alzheimer's affects her visual perception, she sometimes perceives her surroundings differently. To keep Kay safe, caregivers must hold her hands at all times while walking and point things out.

Key Info:

  • Has Alzheimer's and can portray dementia-related behaviors, which include and not limited to:
    • Behaviors can include anxiety, agitation, confusion, or repeatedly asking for her husband Bob or to go home.
    • She is usually easy to redirect through reassurance.

Key Support Strategies

  • Bathing & Dressing: Kay appreciates assistance with choosing outfits and dressing for the day. She loves to look her best!
  • Bathroom Assistance: She is able to make her needs known but benefits from reminders and assistance locating the toilet in unfamiliar places.
  • Hydration: Caregiver(s) are encouraged to kindly offer fluids especially water during car rides and walks to ensure Kay remains hydrated.
  • Restaurant Support: Kay cannot read restaurant menus. Caregivers pick healthy options from the menu.
    • She loves a variety of soups, salads, and tea (avoid carbonated beverages like soda).
    • Caregivers should assist Kay by reading options or pointing them out.
